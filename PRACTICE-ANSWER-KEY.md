# ENG 1005 · Practice Questions — Answer Key
### Writing About Mystery and Thriller

Answers with brief explanations. (Instructor copy / self-check.)

| # | Answer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **B** (SC) | *While* makes it subordinate — it can't stand alone. |
| 2 | **A** (IC) | Complete thought: subject *inspector* + verb *dusted*. |
| 3 | **B** | An *-ing* phrase with no subject is a fragment; add a subject and a real verb. |
| 4 | **A** | Already a complete sentence (subject + verb + complete thought). |
| 5 | **A** | Comma + *then* joins two complete sentences — a comma splice; use a semicolon. |
| 6 | **A** | Two complete sentences with no punctuation (fused); separate with a semicolon. |
| 7 | **A** | Comma needed after the introductory phrase *Before dawn*. |
| 8 | **B** | The renaming phrase *a single feather* is non-essential — set it off with a **pair** of commas. |
| 9 | **A** | A colon follows a complete sentence to introduce an explanation; *revenge* isn't a full sentence, so not a semicolon. |
| 10 | **B** | Two complete, related sentences → join with a semicolon. |
| 11 | **A** | *Because* shows the cause-and-effect relationship between the two ideas. |
| 12 | **A** | *Although* shows the contrast; option C is a comma splice. |
| 13 | **B** | Three adjectives (*nervous, evasive, dishonest*) — same form, parallel. |
| 14 | **B** | Three *-ing* phrases (*reading, interviewing, running*) — parallel. |
| 15 | **B** | The door is locked, not the detective — keep the modifier next to *door*. |
| 16 | **B** | *Nearly* should sit next to *all* (she questioned nearly all of them). |
| 17 | **B** (were) | With *neither…nor*, the verb agrees with the nearer subject, *suspects* (plural). |
| 18 | **A** (its) | *Committee* is a collective noun acting as one unit → singular *its*. |
| 19 | **B** (me) | After the preposition *between*, use the object pronoun *me*. |
| 20 | **A** (tells) | *Each* is singular, so the verb is singular: *tells*. |

**Scoring:** each question is worth 5%. 18–20 correct = excellent; 14–17 = good,
review the misses; below 14 = revisit the Study Guide section for each topic.
