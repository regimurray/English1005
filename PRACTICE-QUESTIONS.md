# ENG 1005 · Grammar & Mechanics Practice Questions
### Writing About Mystery and Thriller

skills but use different sentences. Circle the letter of the best answer, then

## Clauses (Independent vs. Subordinate)

**1. While the storm raged outside**
- A. Independent clause (IC)
- B. Subordinate clause (SC)

**2. The inspector dusted the doorknob for prints**
- A. Independent clause (IC)
- B. Subordinate clause (SC)

## Sentence Fragments

**3. Creeping silently toward the cellar door.**
- A. Complete sentence; no change needed.
- B. Fragment → The thief was creeping silently toward the cellar door.
- C. Fragment → Creeping silently. Toward the cellar door.
- D. Fragment → Creeping silently toward the cellar door, slowly.

**4. The coroner examined the body twice.**
- A. Complete sentence; no change needed.
- B. Fragment → Because the coroner examined the body twice.
- C. Fragment → The coroner examining the body twice.
- D. Fragment → Examined the body twice.

## Comma Splices & Run-ons

**5. The phone rang twice, then it went dead.**
- A. Comma splice → The phone rang twice; then it went dead.
- B. Correct as written.
- C. Fragment → The phone rang twice. Then.

**6. Footsteps echoed in the hall someone was awake.**
- A. Fused sentence → Footsteps echoed in the hall; someone was awake.
- B. Comma splice → Footsteps echoed in the hall someone, was awake.
- C. Correct as written.

## Commas

**7. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?**
- A. Before dawn, the detectives searched the garden.
- B. Before dawn the detectives, searched the garden.
- C. Before, dawn the detectives searched the garden.

**8. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?**
- A. The clue, a single feather pointed to the aviary.
- B. The clue, a single feather, pointed to the aviary.
- C. The clue a single feather, pointed to the aviary.

## Semicolons & Colons

**9. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?**
- A. The motive was clear: revenge.
- B. The motive was clear; revenge.
- C. The motive was clear revenge.

**10. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?**
- A. The storm cut the phone lines, the guests were trapped.
- B. The storm cut the phone lines; the guests were trapped.
- C. The storm cut the phone lines the guests were trapped.

## Coordination & Subordination

**11. Combine: "The safe was empty. The thief had been there first."**
- A. The safe was empty because the thief had been there first.
- B. The safe was empty, the thief had been there first.
- C. The safe was empty the thief had been there first.

**12. Combine: "She knew the truth. She said nothing."**
- A. Although she knew the truth, she said nothing.
- B. She knew the truth she said nothing.
- C. She knew the truth, she said nothing.

## Parallelism

**13. Which sentence is parallel?**
- A. The suspect was nervous, evasive, and lied often.
- B. The suspect was nervous, evasive, and dishonest.
- C. The suspect was nervous, evasive, and a liar too.

**14. Which sentence is parallel?**
- A. The detective enjoyed reading files, to interview witnesses, and stakeouts.
- B. The detective enjoyed reading files, interviewing witnesses, and running stakeouts.
- C. The detective enjoyed to read files, interviewing witnesses, and stakeouts.

## Misplaced & Dangling Modifiers

**15. Which sentence is clear and correct?**
- A. Locked from the inside, the detective studied the door.
- B. The detective studied the door, which was locked from the inside.
- C. The detective studied, locked from the inside, the door.

**16. Which sentence is clear and correct?**
- A. She nearly questioned all of the guests.
- B. She questioned nearly all of the guests.
- C. She questioned all of the guests nearly.

## Agreement & Pronoun Case

**17. Neither the witness nor the suspects ______ telling the truth.**
- A. was
- B. were

**18. The committee announced ______ decision.**
- A. its
- B. their

**19. The reward was split between her and ______.**
- A. I
- B. me

**20. Each of the clues ______ a story.**
- A. tells
- B. tell
