## Section C — Comma Splices and Fused Sentences (10%)
For each item, choose the answer that correctly labels the error — **comma splice (CS)** or **fused/run-on (FS)** — and gives a correct revision.

**1. The lights went out, no one screamed.**
- A. Comma splice → The lights went out, but no one screamed.
- B. Fused sentence → The lights went out no one screamed.
- C. Comma splice → The lights went out, no one, screamed.
- D. Correct as written.

**2. The clock struck twelve the body was discovered.**
- A. Comma splice → The clock struck twelve, the body was discovered.
- B. Fused sentence → When the clock struck twelve, the body was discovered.
- C. Fused sentence → The clock struck twelve the body, was discovered.
- D. Correct as written.

**3. She suspected the gardener he had motive and opportunity.**
- A. Comma splice → She suspected the gardener, he had motive and opportunity.
- B. Fused sentence → She suspected the gardener; he had motive and opportunity.
- C. Fused sentence → She suspected the gardener he had, motive and opportunity.
- D. Correct as written.

**4. The note was unsigned, it pointed to an inside job.**
- A. Comma splice → The note was unsigned, but it pointed to an inside job.
- B. Fused sentence → The note was unsigned it pointed to an inside job.
- C. Comma splice → The note was unsigned it, pointed to an inside job.
- D. Correct as written.

**5. Detective Stone reread the letter then she called the station.**
- A. Comma splice → Detective Stone reread the letter, then she called the station.
- B. Fused sentence → Detective Stone reread the letter; then she called the station.
- C. Fused sentence → Detective Stone reread the letter then, she called the station.
- D. Correct as written.

## Section J — Editing Passage (10%)
Read the original paragraph, then answer the questions about how to correct it.

She noticed a torn glove a muddy boot and footprints leading to the library. Which seemed important. The host insisted that no one leave however the front door stood wide open.
